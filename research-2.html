The Patients

The HR-pQCT, or High Resolution Peripheral Quantative Computed Tomography, captures extremely high quality CT scans of bone. The opening, however, is only large enough for an arm or a leg to fit into so it can only scan bone at the ends of the extremeties.

The Contours

On these images we can see the bone has been separated into it's two main regions of interest: the outer cortical region and the inner trabecular region. This separation is acheived by drawing the border lines around the cortex, also known as contouring the scan. It is both an automatic and a manual process because the computer-generated contours often need to be corrected by hand.

The Analyses

CRTX data extraction computes statistics about the pores within the cortex, including the percentage of bone that is pores and the density of the cortex

The Purpose

The MQIR lab with the UCSF Department of Radiology uses the HR-pQCT to determine the porosity levels of different groups of people. These cohorts range from HIV positive men to Type-II diabetics to patients who have undergone Gastric Bypass surgery. If higher porosity is detected in a group's bones, those patients could unknowingly be at risk for a fracture, often of the hip or vertebrae. If the medical community is informed of the damage that certain afflictions (or the medications used to treat the afflictions) cause to the skeleton, patient care can be adjusted to counteract or avoid the effects.
